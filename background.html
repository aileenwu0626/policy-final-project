Background and Problem Statement

Background

In terms of being transparent, the government does generally look to increase it to improve trust and legitimacy for its reputation and duty towards its people. Big determiners in the release of information are the fear of political backlash and how it could be used. Major concerns include general misuse, endangerment of national security, and the possibility of information being used against the government. Citizens and other civil society parties look to use the data to stay informed, hold the government accountable, and utilize the FOI laws for improved awareness and tackle governmental issues. Concerned with outdated or biased information reaching them, governmental data transparency is important because it ensures accuracy and integrity. Researchers, the private sector, and policymakers seek to utilize accurate data for their respective interests in developing products of intellect, information, policy, and improvement.

Problem Statement

Recently, controversial governmental policies and calculated bills have been causing tension in its foundations, along with the political climate, social movements, and the public’s reliance on its activities. With so much bias embedded behind every agenda, every intention, the trust the people have in their national higher-ups has decreased significantly in the past decade. Data collection and sources are hidden from the public eye, allowing for one-sided political behavior and corruption to pursue. The citizens are also left less educated in their governmental affairs and in crucial metrics than they should be, causing them to form uneducated stances and resentment, further dissociating them from the government’s true intentions and misconstruing facts.

During the earlier weeks of President Trump’s administration, a wide range of important information from the Census Bureau, CDC, and EPA, ranging from webpages, datasets, and mapping tools, has been taken down (Munson, 2025), increasing concerns about the government's reliability in disclosing federal data collection and integrity. One of the most damaging incidents of data removal has been the Trump Administration’s attempt to remove federal data on gender identity for the sake of suppressing DEI initiatives. Surveys, resources, archives, platforms, and clear agendas have been lost in the process. It is hard for the public to recover its trust because a major factor is the government’s ability to be clear about what they do and have.

Scope and Significance

It’s incredibly vital for federal information and statistics to be reliable to the citizens of a nation. A wide scope of coordination, planning, researching, forecasting, and large-scale projects is based on the integrity of the people’s information. Thus, finding, implementing, and enforcing a clear expression of open government data transparency for the benefit of the nation’s trust, progress, and safety should be addressed immediately.
