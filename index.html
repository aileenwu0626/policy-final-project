Project Title

Open Government Data: Transparency and Accountability

Executive Summary

Implementing open government data refers to the intentional release of non-sensitive national information from government datasets, agendas, and applications for the general public to access freely. With data transparency, the government creates an opportunity for improving citizen engagement and assisting experts with informed, scientific innovations and solutions. Politically, the act models the integrity of action and institutes accurate, evidence-based policymaking.

Citizens will be able to recognize and scrutinize governmental activities that are wrong from the publicâ€™s point of view. Simply making decisions on the country without the perspectives of the people can lead to historically detrimental actions and unfair effects. Transparency of data gives the power back to its citizens, allowing them to make worthwhile inputs and voice informed interest-based agendas. Open government data also provides advantages for entrepreneurs and economic prosperity, as well as researchers who can utilize the data and provide accurate analysis on future policy and innovations. With access to truthful trends, patterns, and activities, no matter the nature, specialists will be able to pull together processes and functions across various sectors that would curate improved outcomes from such a wide range of useful data.

It is crucial that the nation can frame sensible open data accessibility on governmental findings, implementing a healthier and efficient path towards national progress and power-responsible honesty.
